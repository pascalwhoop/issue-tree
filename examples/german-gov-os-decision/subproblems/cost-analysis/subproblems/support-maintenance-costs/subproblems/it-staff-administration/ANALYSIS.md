# Analysis: IT Staff and Administration Costs

## Problem Understanding

This problem requires quantifying the human resource costs for managing and administering IT infrastructure at German government scale:
- 500,000 workstations
- 50,000 servers
- Comparing Linux vs Windows platforms

## Key Questions to Research

1. **Staff-to-Device Ratios**: What are typical ratios for enterprise environments?
   - Workstation administration ratios
   - Server administration ratios
   - How do they differ between Linux and Windows?
   - Impact of automation and modern management tools

2. **IT Role Categories and Salary Data (Germany)**:
   - System administrators (junior, mid-level, senior)
   - DevOps engineers
   - Infrastructure engineers
   - Average salaries in German public sector / enterprise

3. **Skill Availability and Premium**:
   - Linux skills vs Windows skills in German market
   - Hiring difficulty and salary premiums
   - Training requirements

4. **Automation and Efficiency Factors**:
   - Linux: Ansible, Puppet, Chef, scripting capabilities
   - Windows: PowerShell DSC, SCCM, Group Policy
   - How automation affects staffing needs

5. **Case Studies**:
   - Large-scale government or enterprise deployments
   - Documented staffing levels for similar scale operations

## Research Approach

1. Search for enterprise IT staffing benchmarks and ratios (2024-2026 data)
2. Find German IT salary data for relevant roles
3. Research Linux vs Windows administration efficiency studies
4. Look for government/public sector case studies
5. Find automation impact on staffing requirements
6. Calculate total staff needs and annual costs for both platforms

## Expected Deliverables

- Staff requirements by role for each platform
- Annual salary costs per role category
- Total annual IT staff costs for Linux vs Windows
- Comparison and key differentiators
