# Analysis: Equity and Distributional Effects

## Problem Understanding

This analysis examines how realized gains taxation versus unrealized gains taxation affects fairness and equity across different taxpayer groups. The key question is not just which system raises more revenue or is administratively simpler, but which system treats taxpayers more fairly across multiple dimensions.

## Why SOLVE (Not Decompose)

While this problem has multiple dimensions (vertical equity, horizontal equity, asset types, life cycles, liquidity, deferral advantages, realization flexibility), these are interconnected aspects of a unified equity analysis. Breaking them into separate sub-problems would fragment what should be a holistic examination of distributional effects. Each dimension informs and relates to the others, so a comprehensive integrated analysis is more appropriate than decomposition.

## Research Plan

I will conduct research to examine:

1. **Vertical Equity Analysis**: How each system affects different wealth and income levels
   - Do high-wealth individuals benefit more from one system?
   - Does either system have progressive or regressive characteristics?
   - How does tax burden distribution change between systems?

2. **Horizontal Equity Analysis**: Whether similar taxpayers are treated similarly
   - Do taxpayers with equal wealth but different asset compositions face equal treatment?
   - Does timing flexibility create horizontal inequities?

3. **Asset Type Effects**: Treatment of liquid vs illiquid assets
   - How does each system handle stocks, bonds, real estate, business equity?
   - Are there distortions favoring certain asset classes?

4. **Life Cycle Effects**: Impact on different life stages
   - Young accumulators vs retirees
   - Working age vs retirement phase
   - Wealth accumulation vs wealth drawdown

5. **Liquidity Constraints**: Ability to pay taxes
   - Cash flow issues with unrealized gains taxation
   - Forced asset sales to pay taxes
   - Impact on illiquid asset holders

6. **Tax Deferral Advantages**: Who benefits from timing differences
   - Value of deferral in realized gains systems
   - Who can exploit deferral most effectively?

7. **Realization Flexibility**: Strategic timing opportunities
   - Tax planning and optimization opportunities
   - Who has sophistication to exploit timing?
   - Lock-in effects and portfolio distortions
