# Sub-Problem: Geopolitical Implications of U.S. Acquisition

## Question
What would be the international diplomatic consequences and geopolitical ramifications of U.S. annexation of Greenland?

## Context
This sub-problem addresses the international relations dimension of the parent question: "Should the United States annex Greenland?"

Key considerations:
- Impact on U.S.-Denmark relations and NATO alliance
- Effect on U.S.-European relations more broadly
- Arctic Council dynamics (U.S., Russia, Canada, Nordic countries)
- Chinese and Russian reactions and Arctic strategy implications
- International precedent for territorial acquisition in 21st century
- Impact on rules-based international order
- Reactions from other Arctic nations (Canada, Norway, Iceland)
- Effect on UN and international law norms

An acquisition attempt wouldn't occur in a vacuum - it would reverberate through international relations and potentially set precedents.

## Acceptance Criteria
A comprehensive answer that:
- Evaluates impact on U.S.-Denmark bilateral relations
- Assesses consequences for NATO alliance cohesion
- Analyzes reactions from Russia and China
- Examines effects on Arctic governance and Arctic Council
- Considers responses from Canada and other Arctic nations
- Evaluates precedent set for territorial sovereignty in 21st century
- Assesses impact on international law and norms
- Considers whether consequences outweigh benefits
- Examines historical parallels and their outcomes
