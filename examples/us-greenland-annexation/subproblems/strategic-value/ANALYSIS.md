# Analysis: Strategic and Military Value of Greenland to the U.S.

## Problem Understanding

This sub-problem asks: What strategic and military benefits would the United States gain from controlling Greenland?

The question focuses specifically on the military and strategic dimensions of potential U.S. control over Greenland, as part of the broader question of whether annexation would be advisable.

## Why I Chose to SOLVE

This is a focused, researchable question that can be answered directly through:
- Analysis of current U.S. military presence and capabilities
- Research on Arctic geopolitical dynamics
- Evaluation of military infrastructure and strategic assets
- Assessment of geopolitical competition in the Arctic region
- Comparison of full sovereignty vs. current access arrangements

This does not require decomposition - it requires thorough strategic analysis and research.

## Research Plan

### 1. Current U.S. Military Presence
- Thule Air Base / Pituffik Space Base capabilities and role
- Existing U.S.-Denmark defense agreements
- Current military infrastructure and personnel

### 2. Arctic Geopolitical Importance
- Climate change and opening Arctic routes
- Russia's Arctic military expansion
- China's Arctic ambitions and "Polar Silk Road"
- NATO's Arctic strategy

### 3. Military and Intelligence Value
- Early warning systems (ballistic missile defense)
- Space surveillance and satellite tracking
- Air and naval bases potential
- Intelligence gathering capabilities
- Arctic domain awareness

### 4. Strategic Geography
- Control of Arctic shipping lanes (Northwest Passage, Northern Sea Route proximity)
- GIUK gap (Greenland-Iceland-UK) for naval operations
- Shortest missile paths between continents
- Airspace control

### 5. Incremental Value Analysis
- What strategic access does the U.S. already have?
- What would full sovereignty add vs. current arrangements?
- Risks and benefits of changing status quo
