## Caveats

1. **Outdoors definition matters**: If "sports and outdoors" primarily means cycling and water sports, Amsterdam wins. If it means hiking/skiing/mountains, Zurich wins decisively.

2. **Social life starting over**: At 1-3 years in Amsterdam, starting over in Zurich is challenging but doable. At 5+ years, this would be a much bigger cost.

3. **Partner's preferences**: This analysis focuses on your priorities. Partner's lifestyle preferences (covered in partner-impact sub-problem) could shift the balance.

4. **Work culture fit**: If you thrive in informal, egalitarian environments, Amsterdam is better. If you prefer formal, structured environments, Zurich fits better.

5. **Seasonal sports**: Zurich's advantage assumes you'll actually use Alps access. If you wouldn't ski/hike regularly, this advantage diminishes.

6. **Integration effort**: Both cities require proactive effort to integrate. Success depends on personal initiative, not just location.

7. **Remote work**: If Evercure role is primarily remote, local culture matters less (though local network still valuable).
