# Sub-Problem: Long-term Quality of Life

## Question

How do Zurich and Amsterdam compare for long-term quality of life, future planning, and stability especially considering potential children and aging?

## Context

This addresses the long-term implications of the decision beyond immediate circumstances. The parent problem is deciding whether to relocate from Amsterdam to Zurich.

**Key factors to analyze:**
- Family planning: childcare, parental leave policies, support systems
- Education system quality (international schools, public schools, higher education)
- Healthcare system quality and accessibility
- Safety and security
- Environmental quality (air, green spaces, cleanliness)
- Political and economic stability
- Retirement and pension implications
- Long-term housing market and investment potential
- Community support for families
- Quality of life rankings and livability indexes

**Personal context:**
- No children yet, but planning for future family
- Haven't purchased property yet (considering buying)
- German national with Italian partner
- Currently 1-3 years in Amsterdam

## Acceptance Criteria

A good answer will:
- Compare family support systems and childcare in both cities
- Assess education quality from kindergarten through university
- Evaluate healthcare system quality and accessibility
- Analyze safety, environment, and livability factors
- Consider long-term financial implications (pensions, property investment)
- Compare community support for young families
- Factor in political/economic stability and future outlook
- Account for quality of life as life circumstances evolve
- Provide clear conclusion on which location better serves long-term quality of life and family planning goals
